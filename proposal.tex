<span id="LC96" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{abstract}</span></span>
<span id="LC97" class="line" lang="tex">Recent years have witnessed the development of different embedded computing systems that incorporate processing, storage, wireless communications, remote administration and sensor devices. The potential of applying wireless sensor network (WSN) and electronic healthcare record (EHR) in the healthcare (HC) industry are tremendous and boundless, while user acceptance would grow exponentially, subject to the fact that users have great confidence in the system. However, the application of WSN in healthcare services may be vulnerable to many attacks and data collected/stored on the healthcare system is required to be securely communicated among users. The security issue of healthcare data must be addressed with great caution and accessibility. Compared with traditional healthcare systems which use paper record systems, adopting and moving on to EHR is a general trend in healthcare industries since EHR systems are considered capable of providing more efficiency, are less error prone and have higher availability. The EHR system security and privacy are related to the issues of confidentiality of protected health information (PHI), the integrity of EHRs and authentication of users. While EHR data are collecting, communicating among different users' access, along with the sharing of the patient health data.<span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC98" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC99" class="line" lang="tex">On the basis of these advanced technologies, and the assumption of providing better quality and cost effective service. In this study, we will develop and build up a prototypical EHR-based healthcare system. The proposed healthcare system will be implemented by: (1) using WSN, a technique to automatically collect patient EHR; (2) taking advantage of EHR repository; and (3) relying on an extensible access control markup language (XACML) to secure communicate EHR among healthcare providers and users. In this proposal, we propose using elliptic curve cryptography (ECC) and elliptic curve digital signature algorithm (ECDSA) algorithms because these algorithms provide high performance and security in WSN compared other asymmetric and symmetric algorithms. But because of the increasing attacks on WSN, some protocols are needed to improve security and efficiency against various attacks. Also, a critical component of the proposed healthcare system is a patient information repository or patient's healthcare database server.  Patients' database in the server needs protection and data management. We propose the use of XACML to manage and access control users (patients and healthcare members) to the database. Such a prototype of the WSN based system would provide better healthcare services with security to various healthcare professionals, including patients. </span>

<span id="LC106" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\section</span><span class="p">{</span>Introduction<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC107" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="c">%\setlength{\baselineskip}{1.6\baselineskip}</span></span>
<span id="LC108" class="line" lang="tex">Wireless sensor network (WSN) technology is widely used in recent years in several applications such as healthcare (HC), military, government security policy, environment, office/home automation, education and transportation <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr9,pr3,pr5<span class="p">}</span>. This type of networks provides comfort and safety to humans by monitoring a specific area without human intervention or the presence <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr1<span class="p">}</span>. An important application that brought the attention of many researchers to sensor networks is healthcare because it is great importance in our lives to reduce the effects of diseases on the health of patients. Providing better healthcare quality of lower cost will be the key aim of all health industry over the next decades.</span>
<span id="LC109" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC110" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span>Healthcare and Information and Communications Technology<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC111" class="line" lang="tex">Many countries have reprioritized the healthcare industry <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>after<span class="p">}</span> the defense and military in terms of budget and strategy <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr44<span class="p">}</span>; it is becoming increasingly evident that advanced Information and Communications Technology (ICT) holds the key to the success of providing better healthcare quality at <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>lower<span class="p">}</span> cost. Healthcare services have become so diversified <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>and demanding<span class="p">}</span> that providers of ICT such as WSN and electronic health record (EHR) systems have to be used in order to deliver efficient but affordable services to the broader healthcare users and communities. These WSNs promise to significantly enhance the care quality over a wide range of clients populations.  </span>
<span id="LC112" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC113" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span>WSN Applications in Healthcare<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC114" class="line" lang="tex">Healthcare is one of the important and sensitive applications that offer quality-of-care for patients. It has been designed to improve the health status of patients and thus reduce the harmful effects of diseases. Moreover, there are many organizations that provide standards for healthcare applications such as Health Level Seven (HL7), Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). These standards are very important <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>when creating a healthcare<span class="p">}</span> application. These applications have become more efficient with the use of WSN; the applications of such kind are known as healthcare wireless sensor networks (HWSN).  <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr1<span class="p">}</span>. By using HWSN, physicians can get information about patients on an ongoing basis, whether at home or in hospital because this information leads to the correct diagnosis and thus the improvement of the patient's condition. Where there are a lot of diseases that require constant monitoring and precise care. For example, HWSN is the best method used by doctors to get patient information, as this technology provides patients with comfort and more care at less cost <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr1<span class="p">}</span>. These applications monitor patient activities without interruption, which leads to improved health condition <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr11<span class="p">}</span>. The architecture of HWSN is shown in Figure <span class="k">\ref</span><span class="p">{</span>fig:archwsn2<span class="p">}</span>. This architecture consists of three layers are sensors, communication equipment and back-end network. This proposal will focus on three aspects of HWSN data:</span>
<span id="LC115" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{itemize}</span></span>
<span id="LC116" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Healthcare data collection<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC117" class="line" lang="tex">In the first aspect, sensors collect data about the patient continuously and send it to the server. The data collected properly helps doctors diagnose the diseases accurately. Also, patients' data collection needs protection from intrusion.</span>
<span id="LC118" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC119" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span><span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>Healthcare data structure<span class="p">}}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC120" class="line" lang="tex">The second aspect is the data storage in the form of tables of a server's database. The data structure for EHR repository should be able to facilitate the sharing of a patient's health information among the healthcare professionals. </span>
<span id="LC121" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC122" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Healthcare data access and privacy<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC123" class="line" lang="tex">The third aspect is to determine who has the right of users (doctor, nurse, general practitioner, pharmacist and government officer) in the access to these tables. All these stages require security and privacy mechanisms to protect the EHR.</span>
<span id="LC124" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\end{itemize}</span></span>

<span id="LC134" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span>Security and Privacy in the Healthcare Applications<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC135" class="line" lang="tex">In this section, we will discuss the risks and security weakness to healthcare applications of WSN. There are several attacks that threaten the WSN used to collecting patient's healthcare data and to the security of the EHR repository. These attacks have been classified into passive and active attacks <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr8<span class="p">}</span>. In all types of passive attacks, an adversary eavesdrops the transmitted packets between network nodes and server. Also, the attacker analyzes these packets to reveal information without change (i.e. trying to break the confidentiality) such as eavesdropping and traffic analysis attacks. <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>The active type<span class="p">}</span> of attack is very harmful to the networks of healthcare applications. An attacker alters data packets and sends them back to their destination such as masquerading, reply, modification and denial of service attacks <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr19<span class="p">}</span>. Also, there is <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>another classification<span class="p">}</span> of attacks on healthcare applications is internal and external attacks. In the internal attacks, the attacker is a member of the network. This type of attacks is more dangerous than external attacks because the internal attacker has the authority to send and receive messages, <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>which means<span class="p">}</span> an attack from the inside is easier. Figure <span class="k">\ref</span> <span class="p">{</span>fig:hackeav<span class="p">}</span> describes the attacks on the WSNs which are used to infiltrate data collected/stored.</span>
<span id="LC136" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC137" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="c">%fig2</span></span>
<span id="LC138" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{figure}</span>[t]</span>
<span id="LC139" class="line" lang="tex">	<span class="k">\centering</span></span>
<span id="LC140" class="line" lang="tex">		<span class="k">\includegraphics</span><span class="na">[width=15cm,height=8cm]</span><span class="p">{</span>pic/hackeav.png<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC141" class="line" lang="tex">	<span class="k">\caption</span><span class="p">{</span>An attack on information security (hacker 1) and device security (hacker 2)<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC142" class="line" lang="tex">	<span class="k">\label</span><span class="p">{</span>fig:hackeav<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC143" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\end{figure}</span></span>
<span id="LC144" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC145" class="line" lang="tex">An attacker listens <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>to information<span class="p">}</span> as it is transferred from the patients' sensors to the server or performs an attack on the server to penetrate the database to get the patients' information, as shown in Figure <span class="k">\ref</span><span class="p">{</span>fig:hackeav<span class="p">}</span>. When the attacker gets the message, he can <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>get information about<span class="p">}</span> the physical location of the patient, ID, timestamps, source address, target address and the medical report sent by the sensors or directed by medical staff. Also, an attacker can get information from the database, such as the patient's name, age, address, type of disease, and the seriousness of the disease. This information allows the attacker to harm the patient in different ways <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr1<span class="p">}</span>. The patient information transmitted through the sensor networks requires complete privacy, especially if patient information through the network does not require the consent of the patient, such as moving data through an emergency case. Therefore, privacy and security issues are very important in healthcare applications. If these applications do not provide adequate security for the patient's information, these applications become useless and unusable because the disclosure of patients' information that may affect their health or even death.</span>
<span id="LC146" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC147" class="line" lang="tex">Most of these attacks can be cancelled <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>when the security<span class="p">}</span> requirements, in particular, confidentiality, authentication and integrity in <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>the network are addressed<span class="p">}</span> properly and appropriately. To prevent attacks of HWSN applications should add the appropriate security services. <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>These services should protect<span class="p">}</span> data from tampering, modification or eavesdropping. The security of the healthcare applications includes:</span>
<span id="LC148" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC149" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{itemize}</span> </span>
<span id="LC150" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Confidentiality<span class="p">}</span> data encrypted through the encryption algorithm to prevent the attacker from seeing explicit data. When the attacker gets the encrypted data, the attacker will not benefit from this data because it is incomprehensible <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr15<span class="p">}</span>. </span>
<span id="LC151" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Authentication and Authorization<span class="p">}</span> authentication service is used to authenticate legitimate users or data in the network to prevent anyone else. This means that if the data is a trusted source in the network it is accepted, but if an unknown source is ignored <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr16<span class="p">}</span>. While in the authorization service, each node in the network has specific sources that can access it. This security requirement is very important in preventing unauthorized persons' access to the sources, for example, providing various privileges among users (doctors, nurses, practitioners and pharmacists) in access to the sources <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr11<span class="p">}</span>. </span>
<span id="LC152" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Integrity<span class="p">}</span> this service is used to ensure that the transmitted data that has not been tampered or edited by the adversary <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr18<span class="p">}</span>. </span>
<span id="LC153" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\end{itemize}</span> </span>
<span id="LC154" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC155" class="line" lang="tex">The following concerns closely related to patient's EHR: </span>
<span id="LC156" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{itemize}</span> </span>
<span id="LC157" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Availability<span class="p">}</span> there are some attacks try to disrupt network services, by sending a large number of messages to the server and thus the network destruction. Network services should be available upon request <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr14<span class="p">}</span>. </span>
<span id="LC158" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Anonymity<span class="p">}</span> this service is to hide or distort the network data as it transfers from the sender to the receiver or vice versa. When using anonymity with the data, the attacker cannot distinguish this data to a specific patient <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr22<span class="p">}</span> <span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Scalability, Forward Secrecy and Backward Secrecy<span class="p">}</span> environments of healthcare applications require expanding the size of the network continuously, but when a node leaves or joins the network, it does not have the right to access and decrypt the encrypted messages in future after leaving the network or previous messages before entering the network. </span>

<span id="LC209" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span><span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>Improvement of Service and Communication in WSN Security and Performance<span class="p">}}</span></span>

Moreover, authors in <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr49, pr54<span class="p">}</span> designed an EHR scheme dependent on many keys and pseudonym property to prevent the link patient definitions with health information. But these schemes <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr46,pr49,pr54<span class="p">}</span> lacked data management among different devices in addition to the complex processes through the use of many keys for authentication.<span class="k">\\</span></span>

Finally, <span class="k">\citeauthor</span><span class="p">{</span>pr53<span class="p">}</span>(<span class="k">\citeyear</span><span class="p">{</span>pr53<span class="p">}</span>) in <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr53<span class="p">}</span> discussed ways of authentication of EHR and EMR. <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>Authors indicated that the EHR has implemented HIPAA standards to protect patients' data<span class="p">}</span>. <span id="LC233" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span>Techniques Proposed to Use in the Study<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC234" class="line" lang="tex">First of all, we list a set of techniques proposed to develop the proposed prototype system as explained in the following.</span>
<span id="LC235" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC236" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{enumerate}</span> </span>
<span id="LC237" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC)<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC238" class="line" lang="tex">ECC is an asymmetric encryption algorithm that depends on the use of the points on the curve to encrypt data. It has been used to provide confidentiality property in the communications network with limited capacity in terms of power and processing. This algorithm was proposed by Neal Koblitz and Victor Miller in 1985 independently <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p14<span class="p">}</span>. It depends on elliptic curve discrete logarithm problem (ECDLP). It is impervious against different attacks when selecting parameters accurately <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p18<span class="p">}</span>, i.e. difficulty obtaining <span class="k">\textit</span><span class="p">{</span>k<span class="p">}</span> from <span class="k">\textit</span><span class="p">{</span>P<span class="p">}</span> and <span class="k">\textit</span><span class="p">{</span>Q<span class="p">}</span> (where <span class="k">\textit</span><span class="p">{</span>k<span class="p">}</span> is an integer and, <span class="k">\textit</span><span class="p">{</span>P<span class="p">}</span> and <span class="k">\textit</span><span class="p">{</span>Q<span class="p">}</span> are two points on the curve) <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p19<span class="p">}</span>. ECC uses small parameters, and therfore makes performing computations quickly, reducing time and storage largely. These features are very important for the constrained-source devices such as WSN that require processing power, memory, bandwidth or power consumption <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p5<span class="p">}</span>.</span>
<span id="LC239" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC240" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Electronic Health Record (EHR)<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC241" class="line" lang="tex">The medical record is a communication tool used to know and review patients' health status among members of the medical staff and patients. This tool is divided into two categories paper and electronic record <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr40<span class="p">}</span>. The paper record is a traditional method that was used in the past to check and record patient information. This type suffered from many problems in dealing with patient data such as accessibility, availability, update, delay, review, errors, data transfer and security. The second type is the EHR; it processes and transmits data rapidly across digital devices. EHR is designed to provide healthcare services continuously and accurately. It has attracted the attention of both the healthcare industry and researchers because it provides the efficiency and effectiveness advantages. This type has several advantages of the first type, such as ease of reviewing the patient to his information, many users are reviewing the medical record at the same time, auto-update and search speed in information retrieval. EHR stores medical records for patients in a digital central database and it manages these records among medical centres, where it provides patients risk assessment depending on the medical reports. In addition, it provides the participation of patient information across the Internet. This information sharing between medical institutions makes it easier for doctors to diagnose and treat patients at any medical centre <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr39<span class="p">}</span>. This type (EHR) also suffers from the problem of security weak during the transfer of data over the Internet or access to data in the server database. Therefore, security mechanisms are considered the cornerstone of the work of EHR systems.</span>
<span id="LC242" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC243" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Extensible Access Control Markup Language (XACML)<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC244" class="line" lang="tex">The most important component in the proposed EHR system is the EHR repository. The repositories contain data in various forms since these systems have difficulties dealing with these different coordinates for data. Therefore, the use of extensible access control (XML) is suitable for the exchange of various data via the Internet. XML is the symbolic language and uses simple and flexible method designed to describe, exchange and management of data across the Internet. It divides the data in the form of useful information through data organization, the purpose of sharing data across different systems and stored in the dataset. Also, XML has several features that make it suitable for data management such as support for Unicode, the representation of computer data structures (trees, records and lists), use a formula read by both human and computer. However, XML should support the security mechanisms to provide different levels of protection of sensitive data in the whole or part of the XML document <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr41<span class="p">}</span>. Access to the data is the great challenge in big data management systems that use different techniques. In addition, the exchange of information over the Internet has become essential, particularly in healthcare applications. But this information needs mechanisms to identify the arrival of unauthorized users to protect patient data.<span class="k">\\</span> XACML standard includes both access control (authorization) and data management based on XML in the different systems <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr42<span class="p">}</span>. It is introduced by the organization for the advancement of structured information standards (OASIS). This standard has many of the features that qualify it for use on the Internet, such as combining policy, combining algorithm, attribute, multiple subjects, policy distribution, implementation independency and obligations <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr43<span class="p">}</span>.</span>
<span id="LC245" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\end{enumerate}</span></span>
<span id="LC246" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC247" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span>To Survey the Efficient Protocols of Providing Security Services on WSN<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC248" class="line" lang="tex">From this point on, we will present our approach. After having done comprehensive reviews on the <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>elliptic curve digital signature algorithm (ECDSA)<span class="p">}</span>, which is reported in our survey paper <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p93<span class="p">}</span>, we have come to the conclusion that the best method to guarantee WSN security is ECDSA that provides the integrity and authentication of the data, i.e. the patient health records. Most previous research focused on the use of ECC/ECDSA and Rivest Shamir Adleman (RSA) algorithms because they offer the best performance, security and suitablity to WSN. Many research papers analyzed the energy, time and data size to public key cryptography (PKC) algorithms (ECC/ECDSA(secure hash algorithm (SHA-1),160-bit) and RSA (SHA-1 or Advanced Encryption Standard (AES),1024-bit)) in sensor nodes of Mica2dot, Mica2, Micaz and TelosB <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p47,p36,p9,p45,p32<span class="p">}</span>. Both algorithms are applicable in WSN, but the ECC/ECDSA offer better performance compared with RSA because it provides small keys with the same level of security. Also, SHA-1 is better in the time and energy than AES <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p9<span class="p">}</span>. Moreover, a comparison can be made between PKC algorithms (RSA, ECC and Multivariate Quadratic Quasigroup (MQQ)) <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr36<span class="p">}</span>. The MQQ provides the highest performance of the ECC and RSA, but ECC presented the best level of security of the PKC in WSN by evaluation of standards institutions of signature and encryption in the world <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span><span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr60,pr59,pr36,pr57,pr58<span class="p">}}</span>.</span>

Servers <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>are used<span class="p">}</span> to store patients' medical records and control access to a database. Collecting patient data through sensor nodes requires protection from the risks and threats <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>(such as internal and external attacks)<span class="p">}</span>. <span id="LC289" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\subsection</span><span class="p">{</span>To Design Protocols to Improve the Security of WSN and EHR<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC290" class="line" lang="tex">In this section, we propose a set of security mechanisms to use in healthcare application based on WSN.</span>
<span id="LC291" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{enumerate}</span></span>
<span id="LC292" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC293" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Quark Hash with ECDSA to Improve the Integrity and Authentication of EHR<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC294" class="line" lang="tex">The hash algorithm is one of the important processes used by the ECDSA algorithm to complete the signing. ECDSA algorithm uses a secure hash algorithm (SHA-1) provided by NIST in 1995 to preserve the message integrity. This algorithm produces a digest 160-bit size with 6122 gate-equivalent (GE), where it needs the complex operations to accomplish digesting message. In digital signatures, the hash algorithm should be collisions <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr29<span class="p">}</span>, preimage and 2 preimage resistance <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr32<span class="p">}</span>. Authors in <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr32,pr30,pr31,pr29<span class="p">}</span> pointed out that this algorithm can suffer from collision and preimage. But SHA-1 is still used in many signature algorithms such as ECDSA. We propose using the Quark hash algorithm for signature in ECDSA rather than the SHA-1. Quark algorithm produces digest 136-bit size with 1379 GE. This algorithm was presented 2010 by authors in <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p7<span class="p">}</span> in order to reduce the memory, energy requirements and resistance to attacks. Quark is faster and lighter than the SHA-1 algorithm. Also, this algorithm is resistant to attacks such as collisions, multicollision, distinguishers, preimage and 2nd preimage.</span>
<span id="LC295" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC296" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Healthcare Data Management by XML to Improve the EHR's Storing/Exchanging<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC297" class="line" lang="tex">Different systems that use a variety of devices require data management to be efficient. In our scheme, we use different devices such as sensors, phones and computers (servers). Therefore, we need a technology that allows for compatibility and data exchange among different devices. We propose the use of XML to manage the WLAN data. This descriptive language enables all devices to read data in a simple and meaningful way. In addition, we suggest using the mechanisms of encryption and signature (ECC/ECDSA) with XML file for the management and protection of patient data at the same time.</span>
<span id="LC298" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC299" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Healthcare Security Using Signencryption to EHR Confidentiality<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC300" class="line" lang="tex">ECDSA algorithm accomplishes operations <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>of the public key, signature generation<span class="p">}</span> and signature verification. The operation of signature verification in ECDSA algorithm consumes more time and energy than a signature generation <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p11, p15<span class="p">}</span>. We assume that we have a hospital care scheme as shown in Figure <span class="k">\ref</span><span class="p">{</span>fig:hospitalcare<span class="p">}</span>. We propose using homomorphic property with signencryption (the signature with encryption) in all sensor nodes. Homomorphic property means the direct computation on encrypted data. Each sensor node signs the message and then encrypts it, and sends it to the cluster head (CH). CH combines all signed and encrypted messages without decrypts or verifies the message. Afterward, CH sends encrypted messages to a nurse. The collection of encrypted messages without decrypt in the CH reduces the transport operations (where the transport operations consume more energy from the computation operations) and thus save time and energy.</span>

<span id="LC309" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC310" class="line" lang="tex">The second part of this scheme is to use 1 scalar multiplication (SM) in signature verification with the intermediate value (IV), where IV with a message encrypted signature is sent from the nurse device to the server (doctor device). IV includes 1 SM's result, ID, time and public key, and encrypts by SHA-1. Signature verification in ECDSA algorithm uses 2 SM. <span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>The SM operation consumes more time and energy in the signature verification <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p17, p46<span class="p">}</span>. This process will reduce the complexity of operations on the server device by calculating 1 SM + IV instead of the 2 SM<span class="p">}</span>. Therefore, we suggest using homomorphic property with all sensor nodes and 1 SM (prime field) of nurse device to the server. The use of the prime field with this approach offers more security than binary field <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>p26<span class="p">}</span>. These processes lead to improved ECDSA performance to secure healthcare applications.</span>
<span id="LC311" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC312" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Anonymity with ECDSA Privacy<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC313" class="line" lang="tex">The ECDSA algorithm is used to perform the integrity of the messages. This algorithm prevents the attacker from changing the message data, since any change in the message will be discovered by the receiver. ECDSA produce the signing of the message (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r,s</span><span class="p">$</span>) where r is the value used to calculate the signature in the process of signature verification and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> is the signature.</span>
<span id="LC314" class="line" lang="tex">The sender signs the message (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">m</span><span class="p">$</span>) by ECDSA and gets (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r,s</span><span class="p">$</span>). The sender sends the message <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">m</span><span class="p">$</span>, <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> to the receiver, who checks the message. There are some of the attacks carried out on the <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> to get the private key <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">d</span><span class="p">$</span>. If the attacker is able to get <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">d</span><span class="p">$</span>, this attacker can produce the same original signatures. Side channel attack (SCA) can penetrate ECDSA signatures and thus get <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">d</span><span class="p">$</span> (Where <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">d</span><span class="p">$</span> is the security in this algorithm, if the attacker discovered the private key, data is easily penetrated). These attacks rely on information leaked to the ephemeral key (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">k</span><span class="p">$</span>) and the value of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> is available publicly. </span>
<span id="LC315" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC316" class="line" lang="tex">We propose adding anonymity property to ECDSA's signature to hide information of a signature's parameters <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> from the attackers through adding two unreal signatures (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="nb">,s</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="p">$</span>) and (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="m">2</span><span class="nb">,s</span><span class="m">2</span><span class="p">$</span>). Moreover, we accomplish this property in the original signature (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r,s</span><span class="p">$</span>) depending on the value of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">k</span><span class="p">$</span>. If the value of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">k</span><span class="p">$</span> is odd, we divide each of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> into three parts, while if the value of k is even, we divide each of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> into four parts and exchange parts (where each of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> have the same number of bits). After the completion of this operation, the value is sent with the message to specify the odd or even division in the receiver. Afterward, the receiver can verify that the message returns the same parts of the <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> to its original place. When using a key length of 384-bit in the case of the division of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span> bits into three parts (odd case), the length of each part is 128-bit. In the case of division into four parts (even case), the length of each part is 96-bit. When using a key length of 521-bit, we add padding 19-bit for a total of bits is 540-bit. Therefore, the length of each part in the odd case is a 180-bit while in the even case is 135-bit. In both odd and even cases to the value of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">k</span><span class="p">$</span> is replacing two parts between <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span>. This mechanism prevents attacks from penetrating ECDSA signature because the attacker when he gets on the value of <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span>, he has not the original value for <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">r</span><span class="p">$</span> and <span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">s</span><span class="p">$</span>. Then, this attacker cannot derive the private key and thereby protect patient's data from the change.</span>

<span id="LC319" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> <span class="k">\textbf</span><span class="p">{</span>Access Control with EHR Database to Improve the Authorization of Healthcare Users<span class="p">}</span><span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC320" class="line" lang="tex">The other important part of healthcare applications is to protect patient's data stored in the server database. These data need protection mechanisms of internal and external attackers. Therefore, patients' database in the server requires defining the access privileges of each user (nurse, doctor, pharmacist, practitioner) wants access to this data. The access control (AC) property only allows authorized users access to certain data. AC provides confidentiality in the EHR by restricting access rights for authorized users <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr27<span class="p">}</span>. </span>
<span id="LC321" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC322" class="line" lang="tex">We propose to merge two models role-based (RBAC) and attribute-based access control (ABAC) to specify the role of each user with a specific task and attributes that grant more privileges. We propose the use of features XACML and anonymity to protect patient's data from illegal and unauthorized access. Initially, users (employees or patients) should be authenticated to the HWSN application to prevent external attackers through the use of signencryption (ECC/ECDSA). The next stage is the use of XACML to determine the information that the user can access. At this stage, our application involves identifying the capabilities of each user (reading, writing), to identify access each user to certain data and define the tasks of each user. Suppose the patient's information is stored in the databases and only the hospital administrator has the authority to access all information. We propose to add an anonymity property with XACML by concealing some fields for some users. We assume the use of three databases (patients' identifications, the health information of the patients and professionals' identifications). Each patient has a number and pseudonym in the central database in addition to the use of the concept of multiple definitions of the various diseases for the one pseudonym. For example, if a patient is suffering from several diseases, there is definition for each disease, where the patient number, health institution number (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">h</span><span class="p">_</span><span class="nb">i</span><span class="p">$</span>), the central pseudonym of the patient (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">p</span><span class="p">_</span><span class="nb">i</span><span class="p">$</span>) with a pseudonym for each disease (<span class="p">$</span><span class="nb">d</span><span class="p">_</span><span class="nb">i</span><span class="p">$</span>) are encrypted. This means that the patient's definition differs whether within the one health institution or many health institutions. In our scheme, we propose encryption of the properties and personal information in XACML file. XACML defines the policies and the semantic to apply those policies. XACML formats are used for request and response between the entities policy enforcement point (PEP) and policy decision point (PDP) to determine access control of the source <span class="k">\cite</span><span class="p">{</span>pr42<span class="p">}</span> (as shown in Figure <span class="k">\ref</span><span class="p">{</span>fig:xacmlops<span class="p">}</span>). Furthermore, XACML is composed of policies and rules. Policies define the applied of request, while the rules governing limitations for the applied of XACML. The request consists of attributes associated with the request sender and the response contains the decision (permit, deny, not applicable and indeterminate).</span>

<span id="LC394" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\section</span><span class="p">{</span>Preliminary Results<span class="p">}</span></span>
<span id="LC395" class="line" lang="tex">Up to now, we have investigated security in healthcare applications based WSN and found that these applications needed quick and secure algorithms to protect patients' data from intruders. During our study, we found that the best encryption algorithms that provide fast computations, high-level of security, small keys and key distribution are ECC/ECDSA algorithms. When we investigated ECC/ECDSA algorithms and we found them suffering from some problems (such as complexity operations of the hash algorithm and SM, and leaked information) and needed some security and performance requirements for application in healthcare based WSN. Therefore, the preliminary results of our research are as the following.</span>
<span id="LC396" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC397" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="nt">\begin{itemize}</span></span>
<span id="LC398" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> Survey paper for ECC/ECDSA algorithms.<span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC399" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>We have investigated asymmetric cryptographic algorithms during our survey paper. We found that ECC/ECDSA is the best algorithm to be used with source-restricted devices in terms of security and efficiency. This step is very important in our research project of building a healthcare application based on WSN<span class="p">}</span>.</span>
<span id="LC400" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC401" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> Implementation of mobile ad hoc network (MANET) in NS3 (C++).<span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC402" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>Our research project is a MANET network consisting of sensors and computers. We implemented MANET in NS3 as a primitive scheme to implement our proposed protocols/algorithms<span class="p">}</span>.</span>
<span id="LC403" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC404" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> Implementation of Quark algorithm in NS3.<span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC405" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>We implemented the Quark algorithm to integrate with ECDSA. After completing this protocol in our research project we will test the results of both ECDSA with SHA-1 and ECDSA with Quark on the level of security and efficiency<span class="p">}</span>.</span>
<span id="LC406" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
<span id="LC407" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\item</span> Implementation ECDSA in C++ language.<span class="k">\\</span></span>
<span id="LC408" class="line" lang="tex"><span class="k">\hlyellow</span><span class="p">{</span>We implemented the ECDSA algorithm in C ++ and will implement it in NS3 to assess security level, energy consumption, data size, delay and time when signing and verifying patient data as the basic algorithm in our scheme<span class="p">}</span>.</span>
<span id="LC409" class="line" lang="tex"></span>
